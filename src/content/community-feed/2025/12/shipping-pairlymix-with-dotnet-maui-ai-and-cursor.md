Mikhail Petrusheuski spent 15+ years on enterprise .NET backends — payment systems, insurance platforms, high-throughput APIs. After writing extensively about .NET 10, AI-assisted development, and Cursor, he decided to put his own advice to the test by building and shipping a real app.

## What you'll learn

- How an enterprise .NET developer approached building a consumer mobile app with .NET MAUI
- Using AI-assisted development workflows with Cursor throughout the build process
- Shipping PairlyMix — an AI cocktail and food pairing app — to both the App Store and Google Play
- Practical patterns you can reuse if you're planning to ship your own .NET MAUI app
- The realities of going from "another architecture diagram" to a product people can actually install
